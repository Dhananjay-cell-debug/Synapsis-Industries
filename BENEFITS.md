# SYNAPSIS vs TRADITIONAL — CORE DIFFERENTIATORS

> The four structural advantages of the Vark Engagement Protocol
> over how most freelancers and agencies operate.

---

## 1. Miscommunication & Backlash → Full Transparency + Data Trail

**Traditional problem:**
Clients and developers argue because nothing is documented. Instructions are verbal, expectations are assumed, and when something goes wrong — nobody has receipts.

**Synapsis fix:**
Every decision, message, approval, and change request lives inside the dashboard. Full conversation trail. No "you never told me that."

---

## 2. Clumsy, Unorganised Process → Extreme Structure & Efficiency

**Traditional problem:**
Work happens in a chaotic loop. Files shared over WhatsApp, feedback in email, invoices on Google Docs, status unknown. Nobody knows what phase they're in.

**Synapsis fix:**
The Vark Engagement Protocol is a gated phase system. Every step is defined, sequenced, and logged. Both sides always know exactly where the project stands.

---

## 3. Client Left in the Dark → Live Phase Tracking System

**Traditional problem:**
Client has no visibility. They're just waiting and hoping. The only updates they get are when they chase the developer.

**Synapsis fix:**
The client dashboard shows real-time phase status at all times. No chasing required. Every phase has a visible entry and exit condition.

---

## 4. Endless Free Revisions → Structured Change Orders

**Traditional problem:**
Scope creep kills projects. "Just one more change" turns into months of unpaid rework. Developers burn out. Clients feel entitled.

**Synapsis fix:**
Two revision rounds are included and documented. Anything beyond scope triggers a formal Change Order — with impact on cost and timeline shown clearly before approval.

---

## Summary

| Problem (Traditional) | Solution (Synapsis) |
|---|---|
| Miscommunication & Backlash | Full Transparency + Data Trail |
| Clumsy, Unorganised Process | Extreme Structure & Efficiency |
| Client Left in the Dark | Live Phase Tracking System |
